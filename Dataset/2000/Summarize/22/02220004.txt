'India committed to minimum deterrent'

Responding to comments by a top U.S adviser to the President on non-proliferation , Mr. John Holum , the Foreign Office spokesman said that India 's dialogue with Washington and others was premised on the possession of a credible minimum nuclear deterrent by New Delhi .
Mr. Holum while participating in a State department Worldnet programme reportedly said that the U.S was opposed to new tests by India but would accept the retention of a nuclear deterrent by New Delhi .
